\subparagraph*{Another More Intrinsic Definition}

$S=\bigcup_{\alpha\in\Lambda}U_\alpha$, $U_\alpha$ is an open piece of $S$, and $\overrightarrow{r_\alpha}:\,\Omega_\alpha\to S$, $(u_\alpha,v_\alpha)\mapsto\overrightarrow{r_\alpha}(u_\alpha,v_\alpha)$ is a local parametrization. If $U_\alpha\cap U_\beta\neq\emptyset$, we have $r_\beta^{-1}\cdot r_\alpha:\,r_\alpha^{-1}(U_\alpha\cap U_\beta)\to r_\beta^{-1}(U_\alpha\cap U_\beta)$, $(u_\alpha,v_\alpha)\mapsto(u_\beta,v_\beta)$, called transition map. If $\frac{\partial(u_\beta,v_\beta)}{\partial(u_\alpha,v_\alpha)}>0$, we call $U_\alpha$ and $U_\beta$ are compatible.

In $\mathbb{R}^3$,\quad\raisebox{-0.5\height}{\includegraphics[height=4cm]{chapters/11/04/0103}}\quad$S\subseteq\mathbb{R}^3$,

\begin{align*}
    r_\alpha:\,\Omega_\alpha & \to \mathbb{R}^3                                                        \\
    (u_\alpha,v_\alpha)      & \mapsto(x(u_\alpha,v_\alpha),y(u_\alpha,v_\alpha),z(u_\alpha,v_\alpha))
\end{align*}

$\implies$ Tangent vectors $\frac{\partial r_\alpha}{\partial u_\alpha}$, $\frac{\partial r_\alpha}{\partial v_\alpha}$ on $S$, and a normal vector $\frac{\partial r_\alpha}{\partial u_\alpha}\times\frac{\partial r_\alpha}{\partial v_\alpha}$

On $U_\alpha\cap U_\beta$, $\begin{cases}
        \frac{\partial r_\alpha}{\partial u_\alpha}=\frac{\partial r_\beta}{\partial u_\beta}\cdot\frac{\partial u_\beta}{\partial u_\alpha}+\frac{\partial r_\beta}{\partial v_\beta}\cdot\frac{\partial v_\beta}{\partial u_\alpha} \\
        \frac{\partial r_\alpha}{\partial v_\alpha}=\frac{\partial r_\beta}{\partial u_\beta}\cdot\frac{\partial u_\beta}{\partial v_\alpha}+\frac{\partial r_\beta}{\partial v_\beta}\cdot\frac{\partial v_\beta}{\partial v_\alpha}
    \end{cases}\implies\frac{\partial r_\alpha}{\partial u_\alpha}\times\frac{\partial r_\alpha}{\partial v_\alpha}=\frac{\partial r_\beta}{\partial u_\beta}\times\frac{\partial r_\beta}{\partial v_\beta}\cdot\frac{\partial(u_\beta,v_\beta)}{\partial(u_\alpha,v_\alpha)}$

If $(U_\alpha,r_\alpha)$ and $(U_\beta,r_\beta)$ are compatible, i.e. $\frac{\partial(u_\beta,v_\beta)}{\partial(u_\alpha,v_\alpha)}>0$, then
$$\frac{\frac{\partial r_\alpha}{\partial u_\alpha}\times\frac{\partial r_\alpha}{\partial v_\alpha}}{\left|\frac{\partial r_\alpha}{\partial u_\alpha}\times\frac{\partial r_\alpha}{\partial v_\alpha}\right|}=\frac{\frac{\partial r_\beta}{\partial u_\beta}\times\frac{\partial r_\beta}{\partial v_\beta}}{\left|\frac{\partial r_\beta}{\partial u_\beta}\times\frac{\partial r_\beta}{\partial v_\beta}\right|}$$
$\implies$ A continuous unit normal vector field.
